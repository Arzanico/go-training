Moor's law says that the density of transistor will double every 2 years.
Not a days this is not longer valid, because few reasons:
1 Temperature increases as power increases.
2 Power increases as transistor density increases.
3 Voltage scaling is limited due to noise or threshold voltage.
4 Voltage scaling reduces (dynamic) power consumption

In the other hand, the chips manufacters have changed the aproach of making chips more powerful by adding cores in the same chips.
